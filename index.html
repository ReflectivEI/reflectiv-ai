Ethics, Privacy & Governance

ReflectivAI is built for life-sciences training. We avoid PHI by default and apply strict safeguards when customers choose to integrate real data.

Data privacy & security
  • PHI off by default; training uses synthetic or de-identified data.
  • When enabled, we operate under HIPAA controls with signed BAA, encryption in transit/at rest, role-based access, and audit logs.
Informed consent
  • If real patient data powers enablement, customers obtain explicit consent and offer an opt-out.
Algorithmic bias & fairness
  • Bias testing pre- and post-deployment; monitor disparate performance and publish remediation steps.
Transparency & explainability
  • In-product recommendations include reason codes; we publish model cards and change logs.
Human oversight & accountability
  • Human reviewers curate scenarios and rubrics; escalation paths and roles are clearly defined.

Frequently Asked Questions

What sets ReflectivAI apart?
AI simulations and analytics paired with subtle EI coaching cues—within strict life-sciences compliance guardrails.
Does it replace human coaching?
No. ReflectivAI scales practice and insights; managers remain essential to interpret data and personalize development.
How do you handle PHI?
PHI is off by default. When enabled, we use HIPAA controls (minimum-necessary scope, encryption, audit logging) and a signed BAA.

Objectives: preventive care, chronic disease management. Objections: time, formulary. Questions: safety, efficacy, guidelines.

Nurse Practitioner (NP)

Objectives: education, adherence. Objections: workload, reimbursement. Questions: dosing, side-effects, resources.

Physician Assistant (PA)

Objectives: collaborative care, triage. Objections: scope, admin burden. Questions: authority, samples.

Infectious Disease Specialist

Objectives: evidence translation, stewardship. Objections: resistance, DDIs. Questions: RWD, emerging therapies.

Oncologist

Objectives: precision medicine. Objections: cost, QoL. Questions: trial eligibility, biomarkers.

Pulmonologist

Objectives: respiratory function, readmissions. Objections: workflow. Questions: eligibility, DDI, initiation timing.

Cardiologist

Objectives: CV outcomes, GDMT adherence. Objections: polypharmacy, renal thresholds. Questions: endpoints, workflows.
