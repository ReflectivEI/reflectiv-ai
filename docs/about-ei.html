About Emotional Intelligence Scoring

ReflectivAI's Emotional Intelligence (EI) framework helps sales representatives understand and improve their interactions with healthcare professionals by providing real-time feedback across five key dimensions.

How EI Scoring Works

Each conversation is evaluated on a 1-5 scale across five dimensions. The scores reflect how well the representative demonstrated emotional intelligence in their interaction:

Dimension What It Measures Scoring Guide
Empathy Understanding and acknowledging the HCP's perspective, concerns, and emotional state 4-5: Strong acknowledgment
3: Adequate recognition
1-2: Dismissive or absent
Discovery Asking relevant, open-ended questions to understand HCP needs and workflows 4-5: Insightful questions
3: Basic inquiry
1-2: No discovery or closed questions
Compliance Adhering to regulations, ethical standards, and respecting HCP boundaries 4-5: Exemplary compliance
3: Acceptable adherence
1-2: Violations or pressure
Clarity Communicating product information clearly, concisely, and relevantly 4-5: Crystal clear
3: Understandable
1-2: Confusing or verbose
Accuracy Providing correct, evidence-based information aligned with clinical guidelines 4-5: Precise & evidence-based
3: Generally accurate
1-2: Inaccurate or misleading

Understanding Your EI Summary

The Emotional Intelligence Summary panel appears in the coach feedback area after each simulated conversation. It includes:

  • Score Pills: Visual indicators showing your performance in each dimension
    • Green (4-5): Strong performance
    • Orange (3): Acceptable, room for improvement
    • Red (1-2): Needs significant improvement
  • Action Tips: Up to 3 specific, actionable recommendations for improvement
  • Rubric Version: Links to the current scoring methodology

How to Improve Your EI Scores

Empathy

  • Acknowledge the HCP's time constraints and workload
  • Validate their concerns before offering solutions
  • Use phrases like "I understand that..." or "That makes sense given..."

Discovery

  • Ask open-ended questions: "How do you currently manage...?"
  • Listen actively before presenting product information
  • Explore patient population characteristics and workflow challenges

Compliance

  • Never pressure for prescriptions or commitments
  • Respect "No" and time boundaries
  • Ensure all claims are supported by approved materials

Clarity

  • Use concise, jargon-free language
  • Organize information logically (indication → efficacy → safety)
  • Adapt detail level to HCP's expressed needs and time

Accuracy

  • Cite specific clinical data and guidelines
  • Never overstate or speculate beyond evidence
  • Reference prescribing information when appropriate
