Emotional Intelligence Scoring Guide

Understanding Your Performance Metrics

How Scores Are Calculated

ReflectivAI evaluates your interactions across six key dimensions of emotional intelligence and sales effectiveness. Each metric is scored on a scale of 0-5, where:

  • 0-1: Needs significant improvement
  • 2-3: Developing - on the right track
  • 4-5: Excellent - demonstrating mastery

Your composite score combines all six metrics to provide an overall assessment of your conversation quality.

✓
Accuracy
Measures how well your statements align with approved product labeling, clinical guidelines, and scientific evidence. Accuracy ensures you're providing HCPs with reliable, evidence-based information.
Score Criteria
5 All claims are label-aligned with appropriate citations. No off-label suggestions.
4 Claims are mostly accurate with proper context. Minor improvements possible.
3 Generally accurate but lacks specific citations or contains vague statements.
2 Contains one or more statements that need verification or clarification.
0-1 Off-label claims, contradicts guidelines, or contains factual errors.

Best Practices:

  • Always cite specific studies, guidelines, or label information
  • Use phrases like "According to the FDA label..." or "The DISCOVER trial showed..."
  • Avoid making claims beyond approved indications
  • When unsure, refer to official prescribing information
⚖
Compliance
Evaluates adherence to regulatory guidelines, ethical standards, and company policies. High compliance scores reflect professional integrity and risk mitigation.
Score Criteria
5 Fully compliant. All statements on-label. Appropriate disclaimers when needed.
4 Compliant with minor areas for improvement (e.g., could add safety reminder).
3 Generally compliant but lacks complete context or risk disclosure.
2 Contains borderline statements that approach off-label territory.
0-1 Violates compliance standards. Off-label promotion or misleading claims.

Key Compliance Principles:

  • Never promote off-label uses
  • Balance efficacy claims with safety information
  • Respect HCP autonomy in clinical decision-making
  • Document adverse events if reported by HCP
?
Discovery
Measures your ability to ask thoughtful, open-ended questions that uncover HCP needs, concerns, and patient populations. Strong discovery leads to more meaningful, personalized conversations.
Score Criteria
5 Asks 2+ targeted, open-ended questions. Uncovers key insights about HCP's practice.
4 Asks at least one good discovery question. Could probe deeper.
3 Question is present but closed-ended or not highly relevant.
2 Limited discovery. Mostly presenting information rather than exploring.
0-1 No questions asked. Monologue without engagement.

Effective Discovery Questions:

  • "What factors most influence your treatment decisions for high-risk patients?"
  • "How do you currently assess adherence challenges with your PrEP patients?"
  • "Can you walk me through your typical workflow for initiating preventive therapy?"
  • "What concerns have you heard from patients about once-daily regimens?"
◆
Clarity
Assesses how concisely and clearly you communicate. High clarity scores mean your messages are easy to understand, well-organized, and free of unnecessary jargon.
Score Criteria
5 Crystal clear. One idea per sentence. No jargon. Perfectly structured.
4 Clear and understandable with minor verbose moments.
3 Mostly clear but could be more concise or better organized.
2 Somewhat unclear. Multiple ideas per sentence or excessive detail.
0-1 Confusing or rambling. Hard to follow the main point.

Tips for Clarity:

  • Use short, declarative sentences (15-20 words max)
  • Lead with the key point, then add supporting details
  • Avoid acronyms unless widely known (e.g., FDA, CDC)
  • Structure your message: problem → solution → benefit
↔
Objection Handling
Evaluates how effectively you address HCP concerns, skepticism, or barriers. Strong objection handling acknowledges the concern, validates the HCP's perspective, and provides evidence-based responses.
Score Criteria
5 Acknowledges concern, validates HCP perspective, and offers evidence-based solution.
4 Addresses the objection well but could validate more or provide stronger evidence.
3 Responds to objection but doesn't fully address the root concern.
2 Dismisses or minimizes the concern rather than addressing it thoughtfully.
0-1 Ignores objection or becomes defensive.

Objection Handling Framework:

  • Listen: Let the HCP fully express their concern
  • Validate: "I understand why that's important to you..."
  • Clarify: "Can you tell me more about..."
  • Respond: Provide evidence or perspective that addresses the specific concern
  • Confirm: "Does that address your question?"
♥
Empathy
Measures your ability to recognize and respond to the HCP's emotional state, priorities, and constraints. High empathy scores reflect genuine understanding and patient-centered thinking.
Score Criteria
5 Explicitly acknowledges HCP's context, priorities, or challenges. Patient-centered language.
4 Shows empathy but could be more explicit or connect better to patient outcomes.
3 Some awareness of HCP perspective but focus is more product-centric.
2 Minimal empathy. Transactional tone without acknowledging HCP's situation.
0-1 No empathy. Pushy or dismissive of HCP concerns.

Empathetic Language Examples:

  • "I appreciate how busy your clinic is..."
  • "Given your focus on high-risk populations..."
  • "I understand the challenge of balancing efficacy with safety..."
  • "Your patients benefit from your thorough approach to..."
  • "I know adherence is a top concern for you..."
